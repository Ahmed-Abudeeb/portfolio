## Overview

Conducted a comprehensive review of recent advances in the application of metal organic frameworks (MOFs) for carbon dioxide capture. The study critically analyzed structure-performance relationships, adsorption capacities, regeneration efficiencies, and scalability considerations.

## Key Findings

- Examined how different functionalization strategies (amines, polymers, hydroxyl, carboxyl, graphene oxide) enhance adsorption performance and stability
- Discussed the impact of synthesis methods, process parameters, and computational studies on CO₂ uptake
- Identified key challenges and future directions for developing scalable, high-performance sorbents

## Publication

This review was published in **Surfaces and Interfaces** (Elsevier) in 2025.
